The archipelago is a Union Territory and is governed by the Union Government of India. They were also known as Laccadive Islands, although geographically this is only the name of the central subgroup of the group. Lakshadweep comes from Lakshadwipa, which means "one hundred thousand islands" in Sanskrit.[3][4] The islands form the smallest Union Territory of India: their total surface area is just 32 km2 (12 sq mi).
